Module 1: Blueprint for a High-Trust Restoration (Water/Fire/Mold) Brand

The low-price competitor sells a cheaper number. A premium contractor sells confidence before the first tool comes out.

Brand visuals that raise trust

For restoration (water/fire/mold), customers notice emergency-ready branding, clean safety gear, documentation photos, mitigation equipment, calm messaging, and proof that the company can respond fast. These signals make the business feel organized, accountable, and worth a stronger price.

  • Clean, wrapped vehicles
  • Matched employee uniforms
  • Sharp logo placement
  • Photo-worthy jobsite standards
  • Simple professional estimate packets

Brand messaging that sells value

Your USP cannot be “we do good work.” Build a promise customers can understand quickly.

  • Fast response promise
  • Clean-site promise
  • Fixed-price transparency
  • Daily communication
  • Photo-documented work
Positioning statement: We help homeowners and businesses get dependable restoration (water/fire/mold) work through professional crews, clean communication, careful property protection, and results that are documented from start to finish.

Module 2: Site Manners & Field Training

The crew is the brand. Customers may not understand every technical detail, but they understand whether your team made the property feel respected.

Arrival SOP

  • Park neatly and never block driveways, garages, gates, delivery areas, or neighboring access.
  • Introduce the crew leader by name.
  • Confirm the scope before unloading tools.
  • Confirm pets, children, gates, alarms, access areas, and restroom rules.
  • Use clean shoe covers or floor protection when entering finished interiors.

Property Protection SOP

  • Protect floors, walls, furniture, landscaping, driveways, doors, hardware, and customer belongings.
  • Create one clean staging area for tools and materials.
  • Keep cords, hoses, ladders, machines, chemicals, and tools out of walking paths.
  • Photograph protection before messy or risky work begins.

Daily Communication SOP

“Good morning. Today we are working on ________. We expect to complete ________. Is there anything you need access to before we get started?”

Jobsite Etiquette

  • No loud music.
  • No smoking or vaping near the property.
  • No bad language.
  • No personal photos of customer property.
  • Daily trash removal is mandatory.

Printable Daily Crew Leader Checklist

Module 3: Physical Collateral That Wins Local Work

When your crew is visible on a job, nearby property owners are warm prospects. Use that moment.

Five-Around / Ten-Around System

  • Leave door hangers at the 5 closest properties on every active job.
  • For visible exterior work or commercial jobs, expand to 10 nearby properties.
  • Use a QR estimate link, a clear service promise, and one strong before/after image when possible.

Leave-Behind Packet

  • Detailed estimate
  • Proof of insurance
  • Warranty or workmanship standards
  • Service/process sheet
  • Review sheet or testimonial page
  • Business card with QR code

Sign Permission Script

“The project is looking great. Would you be comfortable if we placed a small sign in the yard for a few days? We’ll place it neatly and remove it when the project is finished.”

Module 6: The 5-Star Review System

Reviews are not luck. They are a process.

Best Time to Ask

  • After the customer says they are happy.
  • During the final walkthrough.
  • After the site is clean.
  • Before the customer’s excitement fades.
“I’m glad you’re happy with the work. Reviews really help a local trade company like ours. If you feel we earned it, would you mind scanning this card and leaving a quick Google review?”

Reply to Every Review

Thank the customer, mention the service naturally, mention the team/process, and keep the tone human. Do not argue publicly with negative reviews.

Module 8: AI Phone Answering & AI Sales Agents

Missed calls are missed revenue. Many trade companies lose jobs because no one answers quickly enough, especially after hours, during lunch, or while the owner is in the field.

  • AI phone agents can answer calls, collect customer details, qualify leads, and book appointments.
  • Some AI agents can also provide sales support by explaining services, setting expectations, and following up with leads.
  • Pricing often ranges from about $50 to $450 per month depending on employee count, call volume, features, and integrations.
  • Use AI to support your team, not to hide from customers. Complex or upset customers should still reach a real person quickly.
Rule: If the phone rings, the business must respond. If the business cannot answer, the system must answer.

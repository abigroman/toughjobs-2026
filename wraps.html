Why it matters

A name people
can't see isn't
marketing.

You spent real thought on your business name. But a name only works when it's in front of people. Your trucks are already on the road eight hours a day — wrapping them is how you turn a fixed cost into a moving billboard.

A good business name

Gets you remembered

Sharp, memorable, easy to say. It earns trust once a customer already knows you exist. But a name sitting on a bare white van does nothing — nobody can read what they never notice.

A vehicle wrap

Gets you seen

It puts that name — plus your trade, phone number and colors — in front of tens of thousands of drivers and neighbors every single day, in the exact towns you serve. The name and the wrap are two halves of the same job.

The numbers

Impossible to ignore.

Vehicle graphics aren't a nice-to-have — they're the highest-return advertising medium a local trade can buy. The research has been saying so for decades.

01

Up to 70,000 views a day

A single wrapped work truck driving a normal route is seen by 30,000–70,000 people every day — more eyes than most billboards get.

02

The lowest cost per view

At roughly 15¢ per thousand impressions, vehicle wraps beat radio, TV, print and digital on cost-per-eyeball — by a wide margin.

03

Pays once, works for years

A quality wrap lasts 5–7 years. Buy it once and it advertises every mile, every job, every commute — with no monthly ad bill.

04

15× brand recognition

Fleet graphics lift brand recognition up to fifteen times higher than any other form of advertising, per 3M and outdoor-advertising studies.

05

Local by default

Your truck advertises exactly where you work — the neighborhoods, job sites and stoplights where your next customer already lives.

06

A rolling trust signal

A clean, professionally wrapped vehicle parked in a driveway tells the whole street this is a real, established business worth calling.

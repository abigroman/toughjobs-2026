The bottom line

Reputation management for trades businesses in the Midwest.

Reputation management for contractors is no longer optional. Between 93% and 97% of people read online reviews before choosing a local business, and 86% will hesitate to hire a company with negative reviews. For an electrician, plumber, HVAC tech, roofer, or any home-services pro, your star rating is the storefront — it decides whether a searcher calls you or your competitor. At Toughjobs, we build complete review management for trades businesses across Illinois, Iowa, Missouri, Wisconsin and Indiana, from the Quad Cities and Peoria to Dubuque and anywhere within about three hours of home.

The financial case is overwhelming. Harvard Business School research on Yelp found that every one-star increase in rating drives a 5–9% jump in revenue, and businesses with strong Google reviews see revenue climb roughly 18%. Customers will pay up to 22% more for a company with a good reputation, and shops with 200+ reviews earn 82% more per year than those below average. On the flip side, four or more negative reviews can cost you up to 70% of prospective customers, and it takes around 40 positive experiences to undo a single bad one. Reputation is the highest-leverage marketing you can invest in.

Winning it takes two things working together: trained employees who earn and ask for reviews on every job, and a system that automates the request, monitors every platform, and responds fast. Since 37% of negative reviews come from poor communication rather than bad work, coaching your crew is where reputation is truly won. Toughjobs handles both — the people and the process — so your phone keeps ringing. Below are the questions owners ask us most.

How do you get more Google reviews?

We automate a review request by text and email the moment each job is finished, give your crew QR cards and simple scripts, and follow up politely. Because 68–80% of customers leave a review when asked the right way, consistent asking is the whole game — and we make it automatic.

Can you remove negative reviews?

No one can legitimately delete honest reviews, and you shouldn't want to — a mix of feedback actually builds trust. What we do is respond fast and professionally, resolve the issue, and bury the occasional bad review under a steady flow of new 5-star ones.
